Read the fine print. 10% is the S&P 500's rough long-run average before inflation; the 7% view is what it’s worth in today’s dollars. Real markets don’t return a smooth number — a crash right before age 62 can erase a chunk this straight line never shows. These figures assume the money sits in a tax-sheltered account (like a Roth IRA), pays almost nothing in fees, and — the hard part — that you never sell during the scary years.
