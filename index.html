Read the fine print. 10% is the S&P 500's rough long-run average before inflation. The 6.8% view is the real return after 3% inflation — what the money is worth in today’s dollars (10% ÷ 1.03 − 1 = 6.8%). Real markets don’t return a smooth number — a crash right before age 62 can erase a chunk this straight line never shows. These figures assume the money sits in a tax-sheltered account (like a Roth IRA), pays almost nothing in fees, and that you never sell during the down years. Past returns are an assumption, not a promise.
